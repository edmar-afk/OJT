WHAT IS OJT?

ON-THE-JOB TRAINING

About OJT

A training program for student designed to immerse them in a work environment relevant to their courses in the attempts to learn productivity in, knowledge on, and respect for the workplace.

As discussed, on-the-job training programs are course requirements providing an opportunity to apply the theories, principles and ideas learned in the academe but also enhance the technical knowledge skills and attitude of students towards work necessary for satisfactory job performance.

On-the-job training programs also serve as a venue where students earn experience in career positions relevant to their choice of academic degrees as well as open up other future career choices towards gainful employment

OJT MANUAL GENERAL OBJECTIVES:

The BSIT On-the-Job Training Manual is designed to provide the students, parents/guardian/spouse, partner OJT institutions and OJT Coordinator/Instructor with a complete guide of the IT400 course. Specifically, this manual:

A. Provides the students, parents/guardian/spouse, partner OJT institutions and OJT Coordinator/ Instructor with information regarding policies, procedures, roles and responsibilities.

B. Promotes communication and understanding among the concerned participants in the OJT program. Guides the student trainees efficiently and effectively throughout the OJT experience.

C. Defines the OJT through explanation of its purposes, objectives and procedures.

ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS

1. Must be a 4th year standing BSIT student with no other subjects left to be taken

2. Must submit an Application Form for the Internship Program

3. Must have read, understood and accepted the rules and regulations as stated in this Manual

4. Must have attended the Internship (OJT) Program Orientation Seminar

5. Must sign the Agreement and Consent Form and submit it to the respective Department Chairperson

6. Must enroll and register for BSIT Internship (OJT) Program at the Office of the Registrar and Admissions

7. The minimum required number of hours of training per program should be in compliance with the guidelines mentioned in Section 11 of CMO No. 25, Series of 2015 i.e. 486 (46 hours for Seminars and Trainings and 440 hours for practicum) minimum number of hours of internship as stipulated therein for the BSIT program.



PROCEDURES AND GUIDELINES

1. The student trainee is required to attend the Internship Program Orientation Seminar set by the college and submit the necessary documents:

• Comprehensive Resume
• 2x2 ID Picture

2. The student should prepare the required documents addressed to the company concerned, endorsed, by the OJT Coordinator/Adviser and the Program Chairperson of BS Information Technology.

3. The student is responsible for following up their application

4. Upon acceptance by the company/agency, the student must inform the OJT coordinator by submitting any proof of their acceptance (Letter of Acceptance/ Approval from the company).

5. The student submits the waiver form, signed by the parent/s or guardian/s to the OJT coordinator.

6. The student starts at the recommended date. They must record their daily attendance indicating the log-in/log-out time. The supervisor must sign it daily. The Daily Attendance Record (DTR) must be submitted to the practicum coordinator/adviser on a twice a month basis (cut-off period, 15th and 30th) and must be compiled attached in the OJT Experience Summary Report.

7. The student is required to report to his/her practicum coordinator once a week to discuss the problem queries.

8. The student is required to make a progress report, on a day-to-day basis, which is to be submitted weekly to the practicum coordinator/adviser.

9. The student should at all times keep all documents pertaining to their OJT.

10. The student must comply with the general guidelines of the company regarding their own OJT program.

11. The student is required to submit a hardbound of compilation of the accomplishment report as one of the final requirements.

Roles and Responsibilities of Involved Parties

PART 2


STUDENT TRAINEES

1. An approved student/trainee shall abide by the rules and regulations set forth by the institution/company/office where they are undergoing on-the-job training.

2. The student/trainee should wear the appropriate dress code.

3. The student /trainee/ is expected to submit reports and requirements on time.

4. The student/trainee should observe punctuality and attendance in reporting to their assigned training area. They should accomplish the attendance recording sheet Daily Time Record (DTR) noted by the training supervisor.

5. Each student trainee has the following responsibilities within the company:

• Data Encoding
• Technical Support
• Costumer Service
• Program Development
• Office Works (answering phone calls, photocopying, record keeping)
• Technical Support
• Familiarization in the functions/jobs done by the employees handling “Computer or IT Related” work in the industry.

6. Student trainee must always observe discipline and right conduct in performing their task while on training.

7. Student trainee must comply with OJT guidelines and procedures provided by the Practicum Coordinator.

8. They should not engage in gambling, illicit activities, drinking intoxicating beverages and related activities while at work or within the institution/company/office premises.


SCHOOL/DEPARTMENT

1. The school/department shall formulate the on-the-job training policies and guidelines on the selection, placement, monitoring and assessment of student/trainees.

2. The school/department shall assign a regular faculty member, tenured and academically qualified as OJT Coordinator responsible for all aspects of the student internship programs including program implementation, monitoring and evaluation.

3. The school/department shall select the partner institution/company/office and ensure acceptability of training design and venue in order to protect the student/trainee’s interest.

4. The school/department shall develop in collaboration with the duly selected partner institution/company/office a Training Plan, which will form part of training contract, for the student trainee/ intern specifying goals and objectives, knowledge, skills, and competencies that the student trainee should acquire in each training area, assignment and schedule of activities, among others.

5. The school/department shall also monitor and evaluate performance of student/ trainee jointly with the partner institution/company/office based on the agreed training plan.

6. The school/department shall monitor the student/trainee and immediately attend to his/her complaints by coordinating with the partner institution/company/office, CHED and other concerned government authorities if necessary.

7. The school/department also conducts initial visit/ inspection of their training partner organization to ensure safety of students.


PRACTICUM INDUSTRY PARTNER

1. The partner institution/company/office shall be responsible in providing actual on-the-job training (OJT) to the student/trainee.

2. The partner institution/company/office shall be responsible in assigning the student/trainee to any department during the duration of the OJT requiring for exposure to actual work. The OJT schedule shall be determined by the partner institution/company/office in coordination with the student/trainee and the school’s assigned OJT coordinator.

3. Ensures that all the documents needed by the trainee are complete before the start of the training.

4. Work assignments to the student/trainee should be clearly explained by the partner institution/company/office to ensure that the duties, role and responsibilities of the student/trainee is clearly defined and outlined.

5. The partner institution/company/office shall assign a focal person to serve as On-Site Supervisor who will monitor the student/trainee’s overall work performance including demeanor, competence and professionalism.

6. Upon completion of the required number of hours, the Training Coordinator shall issue a certificate of the completion to the trainees.

7. The assigned supervisor shall fill out and sign the OJT Performance Evaluation Forms.


PRACTICUM COORDINATOR/INSTRUCTOR

1. The OJT Coordinator shall be responsible for all aspects of the student internship programs including program implementation, monitoring and evaluation.

2. The OJT Coordinator is expected to review, orient interpret and clarify to the student/trainee the objectives of the on-the-job training program.

3. Assists the Program Chairperson in preparing and implementing the program of activities of the OJT program.

4. Assists the Program Chairperson in the selection of industry partners/practicum partners/venues.

5. Helps in the proper implementation of the Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) between the College and the Industry linkage.

6. Coordinates with the heads/manager of the practicum partners regarding the activities and needs/requirements of the student –trainees relative to the practicum program.

7. Prepare the student trainees to their practicum/training through series of orientations, meetings, conferences and workshops.

8. Conducts regular observation and evaluation of the performance of the student-trainees.

9. Coordinates regularly with the practicum partners regarding the performance of the student-trainees.

10. Rates/Evaluates the performance of the student-trainee based on the evaluation of heads/managers and completion of all requirements as determined by the college.


ON-SITE SUPERVISOR

1. The On-Site Supervisor is responsible in providing the student/trainee with opportunities to work with the specific department or area where they can best apply the theories, principles and ideas learned in academe.

2. They are also responsible in coordinating with the different departments of the partner institution/company/office where the student/trainee will report.

3. They are responsible in supervising the student/trainee for the duration of the on-the-job training program.

4. They will also facilitate the evaluation of the student/trainee’s performance using the agreed upon standard or requirement of the school/college.

5. They will regularly meet with the OJT Coordinator to discuss their evaluation, observation and other issues that may arise for the duration of the on-the-job training program. This will also be an opportunity to discuss the program of the student/trainee.

6. They will prepare the evaluation of the student/trainee, upon completion of the on-the-job training program and submit such evaluation to the OJT Coordinator.


PARENT/GUARDIAN

1. To encourage the student to carry out effectively his/her duties and responsibilities.

2. To share responsibilities for the conduct of the student while in the training program.

3. To be responsible for his safety and conduct of student-learner while travelling to and from school, the OJT site and home.

4. The parents/guardian shall co-sign the training contract to manifest approval or consent to the on-the-job training of their child.

5. The parents/guardian shall also issue a waiver of consent.


DISCIPLINE/PUNISHMENT/PENALTY

Attendance

1. Absences

• Excused -additional 8 hours (to the Internship Program)
• Each Unexcused Absence -additional 16 hours
• After the 5th Unexcused Absence -Repeat the Internship

2. Tardiness (beyond 15 minutes)

• First offense -excused
• Second offense -additional 2 hours
• Third offense -additional 4 hours
• Succeeding offenses -additional 6 hours

Deficiencies, Misbehavior, and Infractions

For Offenses A-E --------------------------------- additional 4 hours

A. Not wearing prescribed uniform
B. No proper grooming
C. Leaving the office premises without permission
D. Making unnecessary noise in the office
E. Doing things not related to work during office hours
(texting, sleeping, playing, reading, etc)

For Offenses F-G -------------------------------- additional 8 hours

F. Improper use and care of office equipment
B. G. Loss of office materials, equipment

For Offenses H-O---------------------------- Repeat the Internship

H. Bringing office documents outside the Company without permission
I. Falsification of documents(false entries in the DTR, false entries in incident reports, logging in/out for another Intern)
J. Insubordination
K. Gambling
L. Intoxication with alcohol or drugs
M. Vandalism to the Company and property of the Company.
N. Unauthorized entry to or use of any Company’s private property
O. Assault, physical or verbal abuse and threats towards the superiors, coordinators and co-interns
P. Violation of Company’s policies
Q. Any other act committed by the Interns, which affect the reputation of the College

Monitoring and Evaluation

PART 3



MONITORING and ATTENDANCE

1. Proper office attire must be worn to work at all times.

2. Proper grooming must be observed at all times.

3. A Daily Time Record must be filled up every day.

4. Absences must be supported with excuse slips (Form 6) and/or medical certificates. The OJT Coordinator must be notified immediately by landline phone/mobile phone or through a letter of absence.

5. Excuse slips must be countersigned by the OJT Coordinator. Otherwise, the absence will be unexcused.


EVALUATION AND GRADING SYSTEM

1. Evaluation and grading of each Student/Trainee will be performed by the OJT Coordinator and the Site Coordinator. The criteria for evaluation are the following:



a. Personal Traits and Qualities = 30%
b. Intellectual and Professional Background = 30%
c. Interpersonal Skills = 30%
d. General Performance = 10%

5. The OJT Coordinator and the Site Coordinator will submit an evaluation at the end of each phase.

6. During the term, all students enrolled in the Internship Program will receive an “INC” mark in their grade. Final grades will be released upon completion of the 486 Internship hours.

LEARNING OBJECTIVES: What do you intend to learn through this experience?

The academic component is to be determined by the student with assistance from the Internship Coordinator and Site Supervisor. It is recommended that two or more learning objectives be identified in each of the following categories, with specific strategies for each objective, plus at least one method of evaluation for each objective:


1. Academic Learning and Application (i.e. related to the ideas, concepts, or theories of your major or minor field(s) of study and ideas or concepts related to the major/minor’s perspective.


2. Skill Development (i.e. skills specific to your academic major/minor or an occupation; and/or general skills such as oral and written communication, critical thinking, problem solving, leadership, technical, etc.)


3. Personal Development (e.g., self-confidence, career awareness, clarification of work and personal values, etc.)
